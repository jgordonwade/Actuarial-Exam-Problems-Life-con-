\JGWitem{C3 Nov 2018, \#15}
For a special 20-year term insurance of 200,000 with level monthly premiums issued to (65), you are given:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] The death benefit is paid six months after the death of the policyholder.
\item[(ii)] Mortality follows the Standard Ultimate Life Table.
\item[(iii)] Woolhouse's formula with two terms is used to calculate $(1/m)$thly and continuous
annuities, and to calculate insurance functions from the annuity values.
\item[(iv)] The monthly net premium is 274.
\item[(v)] $i=0.05$
\end{itemize}
Calculate the net premium reserve at time 10.
\showsol{\bsoln
  The APV of future benefits at time $t=10$ is $200,000\cdot v^{1/2}\Ax*{\term{75}{10}}$ (the $v^{1/2}$ here reflects the 6-month delay in payment)
  and the APV of future premiums at time $t=10$ is $12\cdot 274\ax**{\endow{75}{10}}[(12)],$ so
  \bears
     \tV{10} = 200,000\cdot v^{1/2}\Ax*{\term{75}{10}} - 12\cdot 274\ax**{\endow{75}{10}}[(12)].
  \eears
  Now
  \bears
        \Ax*{\term{75}{10}} &=&  \Ax*{\endow{75}{10}} - \Ex[10]{75} 
        = \left(1-\delta\ax*{\endow{75}{10}}\right) - \Ex[10]{75} 
        = \left(1-\delta\left(\ax*{75} - \Ex[10]{75} \ax*{85}\right)\rule{0pt}{10pt}\right) - \Ex[10]{75} \\
        &\approx& 1-\delta\left(\left(\ax**{75} - 1/2\right)- \Ex[10]{75} \left(\ax**{85}-1/2\right)\rule{0pt}{9pt}\right) - \Ex[10]{75} \\
        &=& 1-\ln(1.05)\left(\left( 10.3178- 1/2)\right)-0.44085  \left(6.7993-1/2\right)\right) - 0.44085 = \underline{0.215630},
  \eears
  and 
  \bears
     \ax**{\endow{75}{10}}[(12)]  = \ax**{75}[(12)] - \Ex[10]{75}\ax**{85}[(12)] 
      &\approx& \left(\ax**{75}-11/24\right) - \Ex[10]{75}\left(\ax**{85} - 11/24\right) \\
      &=& \left( 10.3178- 11/24\right)-0.44085  \left(6.7993-11/24\right) = \underline{7.064052}
  \eears
  so
  \bears
     \tV{10} &=& 200,000\cdot v^{1/2}\Ax*{\term{75}{10}} - 12\cdot 274\ax**{\endow{75}{10}}[(12)] \\
         &=& \dfrac{200,000}{1.05^{1/2}}\cdot 0.215630 - 12\cdot 274\cdot 7.064052 = \boxed{18860.06}
  \eears


\esoln}
