\JGWitem{C3 Nov 2014 \#03} % LTAM Sample Q's (Nov 2019) \#8.14
Patients are classified as Sick (S), Critical (C), or Discharged (D). Transitions occur daily according to the following Markov transition matrix:
\[ \begin{array}{c} \text{(S)}  \\  \text{(C)} \\  \text{(D)} \end{array} \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0.60 & 0.20 & 0.20\\ 0.10 & 0.50 & 0.40 \\ 0.00 & 0.00& 1.00 \end{array}\right]. \]
Calculate the probability that a patient who is classified as Sick today will be classified as Sick three days later.
\showsol{\bsoln
  \[  \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0.60 & 0.20 & 0.20\\ 0.10 & 0.50 & 0.40 \\ 0.00 & 0.00& 1.00 \end{array}\right]^3 
       = \left[\begin{array}{rrr}0.25 & 0.186 & 0.564 \\0.093 & 0.157 & 0.75 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]
  \]
  so the probability that a patient who is classified as Sick today will be classified as Sick three days later is $\boxed{0.25}$ 

  \medskip
  Or another way:
  \begin{center}\begin{tabular}{cr} \rule{30pt}{0pt} Path \rule{30pt}{0pt}& ${\rm Prob}\{\text{Path}\}$  \\ \hline
  \rule{0pt}{22pt}$\text{S}\rightarrow\text{S}\rightarrow{S}\rightarrow{S}$ & $0.6^3 = 0.216 $ \\
  \rule{0pt}{22pt}$\text{S}\rightarrow\text{S}\rightarrow{C}\rightarrow{S}$ & $0.6\cdot 0.2\cdot 0.1 = 0.012 $ \\
  \rule{0pt}{22pt}$\text{S}\rightarrow\text{C}\rightarrow{S}\rightarrow{S}$ & $0.2\cdot 0.1\cdot 0.6 = 0.012 $ \\
  \rule{0pt}{22pt}$\text{S}\rightarrow\text{C}\rightarrow{C}\rightarrow{S}$ & $0.2\cdot 0.5\cdot 0.1 = 0.01 $ \\
   \hline \rule{0pt}{14pt}\rule{80pt}{0pt} Sum: & 0.25
\end{tabular}\end{center}


\esoln}
