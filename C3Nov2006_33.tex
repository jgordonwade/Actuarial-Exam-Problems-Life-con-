\JGWitem{ C3 Nov 2006, \# 33}
You are given:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] $Y$ is the present value random variable for a continuous whole life annuity of $1$ per year on (40).
\item[(ii)] Mortality follows DeMoivre's Law with $\omega =120.$
\item[(iii)] $\delta = 0.05$
\end{itemize}
Calculate the $75^{th}$ percentile of the distribution of $Y.$
\showsol{\bsoln
The present value of a continuous annuity paying 1 per years for $t$ years is
\\ \dsy{\rule{0pt}{20pt}\ax*{\angl{t}}=\int_{z=0}^t e^{-\delta z}\,dz = \dfrac{1-e^{-\delta t}}{\delta},} 
so \dsy{Y = \ax*{\angl{T_{40}}}=\dfrac{1-e^{-\delta  T_{40}}}{\delta}. }
The $75^{th}$ percentile of $Y$ is that value (call it $y_*$) 
for which \dsy{\rule{0pt}{20pt}\Prob{Y\leq y_*}=0.75.} So,
\bears
  0.75 = \Prob{Y\leq y_*} &=& \Prob{\dfrac{1-e^{-\delta T_{40}}}{\delta}\leq y_*}  \\
   &=& \Prob{ T_{40} \leq -\ln(1 - \delta y_*)/\delta } \\
   0.75 &=& \Prob{ T_{40} \leq t_* } \quad\text{ where }t_*= -\ln(1 - \delta y_*)/\delta .
\eears
Since $T_{40}$ is DeMoivre (uniform) on $[0,80]$,
\[  0.75 = \Prob{ T_{40} \leq t_* } \quad \Longrightarrow \quad 0.75 = t_*/80 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad t_*=60. \]


\esoln}
