\JGWitem{May 2007 \#22}
For a special whole-life insurance on (40), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item The death benefit is 1000 for the first 10 years and 2500 thereafter. 
 \item Death benefits are payable at the moment of death. 
 \item $Z$ is the present value random variable.
 \item Mortality follows deMoivre's law with $\omega=100$. 
 \item $\delta=0.10$. 
\end{enumerate}
Calculate $\Prob{Z>700}$

\showsol{\bsoln
Denote by $T$ the future lifetime random variable. It takes values in $(0,60]$. We have:
\[
  Z = \left\{\begin{array}{cl} 
           1000e^{-T/10} & \mbox{if }0<T\leq 10 \\
           2500e^{-T/10} & \mbox{if }10<T\leq 60.
    \end{array}\right.
\]
The variable $Z$ depends piecewise continuously on $T$, and is decreasing everywhere except at $T=10$ where it jumps up. We have 
\bears 
   Z &=& 700 \quad\mbox{ when }T=3.567, \\
  \mbox{and }
   Z &=& 700 \quad\mbox{ when }T=12.73
\eears
So 
\bears
  \Prob{Z>700} &=& \Prob{T\leq 3.567 \quad\mbox{ \underline{or} }\quad 10<T\leq 12.73} \\
     &=& \frac{3.567}{60} + \frac{(12.73-10)}{60} = 0.1049
\eears
\esoln}
