\JGWitem{Nov 2002, \#13}
For a double-decrement table where cause 1 is death and cause 2 is withdrawal, you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Deaths are uniformly distributed over each year of age in the single-decrement table.
\item Withdrawals occur only at the end of each year of age.
\item  $l_x^{(\tau)} = 1000$
\item $q_x^{(2)}=0.40$
\item $d_x^{(1)} = 0.45d_x^{(2)}$
\end{enumerate}
Calculate $p_x^{'(2)}$.
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 0.51
\item[(B)] 0.53
\item[(C)] 0.55
\item[(D)] 0.57
\item[(E)] 0.59
\end{description}}
\showsol{\bsoln
\showMC{\underline{Ans: ()}}

For $t\in[0,1)$, only decrement (1) is in effect and it is uniformly distributed so
\[
   \prepostsubsup{t}{p}{x}{\tau} =  \prepostsubsup{t}{p}{x}{(1)} = \prepostsubsup{t}{p}{x}{'(1)} = 1-tq_x^{'(1)} \quad\mbox{for }t\in [0,1),
 \]
 and $p_x^{(1)} = p_x^{'(1)}$

\bears
   d_x^{(2)}  &=&  l_x^{(\tau)}\cdot q_x^{(2)} = 400 \\
   d_x^{(1)} &=& 0.45\times 400 = 180 \\
   q_x^{(1)} &=& 0.180 \quad\mbox{because decrement (2) can't happen midyear} \\
   d_x^{(\tau)} &=& 580, \\
   q_x^{(\tau)} &=& 0.580 \\
   p_x^{(tau)} &=& 0.420 \\
   q_x^{(2)} &=& 0.400 \\
 \quad  q_x^{(2)} &=& p_x^{(\tau)}q_x^{'(2)}
 \eears

\esoln}
