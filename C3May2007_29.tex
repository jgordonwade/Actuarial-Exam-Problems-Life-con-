\JGWitem{C3 May 2007, 29}
For a special fully discrete, 30-year deferred, annual life annuity-due of 200 on (30), you are given: 
\begin{enumerate}
\item The single benefit premium is refunded without interest at the end of the year of death if death occurs during the deferral period. 
\item Mortality follows the Illustrative Life Table. 
\item $i= 0.06 $
\end{enumerate}
Calculate the single benefit premium for this annuity. 
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 350
\item[(B)] 360
\item[(C)] 370
\item[(D)] 380
\item[(E)] 390
\end{description}}



\showsol{\bsoln
The single benefit premium is the expected value of the benefits.
\bears
   P &=& 200\ax**[30|]{30} + P\Ax{\term{30}{30}}  
   \\&=& 200\left(\Ex[30]{30}\right)\ax**{60} + P\left(\Ax{30}-\Ex[30]{30}\Ax{60}\right) 
   \\&=& 200\left(\Ex[30]{30}\right)\left(\dfrac{1-\Ax{60}}{d}\right) + P\left(\Ax{30}-\Ex[30]{30}\Ax{60}\right) 
\eears
Now $\Ex[30]{30}=\dfrac{\ell_{60}}{\ell_{30}\cdot 1.06^{30}} = \dfrac{8188073}{9501380\cdot 1.06^{30}} = 0.15004415,$
and $\Ax{60}=0.36913104$, so 
\bears
  P &=& 200\cdot 0.15004415\left(\dfrac{1-0.36913104}{0.05660377}\right) + P\left(0.10248356 - 0.15004415\cdot 0.36913104\right)
    \\ P &=& 334.45898  + P\cdot 0.04709761
  \quad\Longrightarrow \boxed{P=350.99}
\eears
\esoln}
