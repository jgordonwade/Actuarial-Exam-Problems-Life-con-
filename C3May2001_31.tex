\JGWitem{May 2001, \#31}
For a special fully discrete 3-year term insurance on (x):
\begin{enumerate}
\item Level benefit premiums are paid at the beginning of each year.
\item \begin{tabular}{|ccc|} \hline 
                           $k$ & $b_{k+1}$ & $q_{x+k}$ \\ \hline
                            0  & 200,000 &  0.03 \\
                            1  & 150,000 &  0.06 \\
                            2  & 100,000 &  0.09 \\
      \hline\end{tabular}
\item $ i = 0.06$
\end{enumerate}
Calculate the initial benefit reserve for year 2.
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 6,500
\item[(B)] 7,500
\item[(C)] 8,100
\item[(D)] 9,400
\item[(E)] 10,300
\end{description}}
\showsol{\bsoln
The initial benefit reserve for year 2 is $\prepostsub{1}{V}{} + \pi$.

\medskip We have to compute $\pi$, and since the benefit changes each year, we have to compute it from first principles.
We have
\begin{center}\begin{tabular}{c|c|c|l}
   k & $q_{x+k}$ & $p_{x+k}$ & $\prepostsub{k|}{q}{x}$ \\ \hline
   0 & $0.03$   & $0.97$   & $0.03$ \\
   0 & $0.06$   & $0.94$   & $(0.97)\cdot(0.06) = 0.0582$ \\
   0 & $0.09$   &          & $(0.97)\cdot(0.94)\cdot(0.09) = 0.082062$
\end{tabular}\end{center}
\bears
   A &=& 100,000\Bigl( 2\cdot(0.03)v + 1.5\cdot(0.0582)v^2 + 1\cdot(0.082062)v^3 = 20,320.13 \\
   \ddot{a} &=& \sum_{k=0}^{3-2} \EEE{k}{x} = 1 + (0.97)v + (0.97)(0.94)v^2 = 2.72659 \\
   \pi &=& A/\ddot{a} = 7452.57 \\
\eears

\medskip
Retrospectively, 
\bears
    \prepostsub{1}{V}{} &=& \frac{1}{E_x}\left( \pi - vq_x\cdot 200,000  \right)  \\
            &=& \frac{1.06}{0.97}\left(  7452.57  - \frac{0.03\times 200,000}{1.06}\right) = 1958.48 \\
            \mbox{\sc ans} = \prepostsub{1}{V}{} + \pi &=& 1958.48 + 7452.57 = \underline{9411.01}
\eears


\esoln }
