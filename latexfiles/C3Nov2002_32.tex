\JGWitem{Nov 2002, \#32}
For a continuously increasing whole life insurance on (x), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
     \item The force of mortaility is constant.
     \item $\delta = 0.06$
     \item $\pretwo{\bar{A}}_{x} = 0.25$
\end{enumerate}
Calculate $(\overline{IA})_x$
\showsol{\bsoln
 Since \dsy{\int_0^\infty \mu e^{-(\delta+\mu)t}\,dt = \dfrac{\mu}{\delta+\mu},}
 we have
 \[ \Ax[][2]{x} = 0.25 = \dfrac{\mu}{2\cdot 0.06+\mu} \quad\Longrightarrow \quad\mu = 0.04\]
 Then 
 \bears     (\overline{IA})_x &=& \int_{t=0}^\infty \mu t e^{-(\delta+\mu) t}\, dt = \dfrac{\mu}{(\mu+\delta)^2} = \dfrac{0.4}{0.1^1} = 4. \eears
\esoln}
