\JGWitem{ C3, Nov 2013, \#10}  % Markov Chain
For a multiple state model, you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item {\em (diagram showing the following:)} Three states, 0,1,2, which are ``Healthy, Disabled, Dead'', and it not not possible to transition from ``Disabled'' back to ``Healthy''.
\item The following forces of transition:
   \begin{enumerate}
       \item $\mu^{01} = 0.02 $
       \item $\mu^{02} = 0.03 $
       \item $\mu^{12} = 0.05 $
   \end{enumerate}
   Calculate the conditional probability that a Healthy life on January 1, 2004 is still
   Healthy on January 1, 2014, given that this person is not Dead on January 1, 2014.
   \end{enumerate}
\showsol{\bsoln
\bears
  \dfrac{d}{dt}\actsymb[t]{\mathbf{P}} \ &=& \actsymb[t]{\mathbf{P}} \ \mathbf{U} = 
  \left[\begin{array}{ccc} \px[t]{}[00] & \px[t]{}[01] & \px[t]{}[02]  \\  \px[t]{}[10] & \px[t]{}[11] & \px[t]{}[12]  \\  \px[t]{}[20] & \px[t]{}[21] & \px[t]{}[22] \end{array}\right]
                                     \left[\begin{array}{ccc} -0.05 & 0.02 & 0.03 \\ 0 & -0.05 & 0.05 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \\
\eears
\bears
  \dfrac{d}{dt}\px[t]{}[00]  &=&  \dfrac{-1}{20}\px[t]{}[00], \qquad \px[0]{}[00]=1, \\
  \dfrac{d}{dt}\px[t]{}[01]  &=&  \dfrac{-1}{20}\px[t]{}[01] + \dfrac{1}{50}\px[t]{}[00], \qquad \px[0]{}[01]=0, \\
  \dfrac{d}{dt}\px[t]{}[02]  &=&  \dfrac{3}{100}\px[t]{}[00] + \dfrac{1}{20}\px[t]{}[01], \qquad \px[0]{}[02]=0.
\eears
We need $\px[10]{}[00]/\left(\px[10]{}[00] + \px[10]{}[01]\right)$
\bears
  \px[t]{}[00]  &=&  e^{-0.05t } \qquad \px[10]{}[00] = \underline{0.606531} \\
  \px[t]{}[01]  &=&  \int_0^t e^{-0.05s}\cdot 0.02\cdot\px[t-s]{}[00]  \,ds = 0.02\cdot \int_0^t e^{-0.05s}e^{-0.05(t-s)}\,ds = 0.02\cdot te^{-0.05t}  \\
  && \qquad \px[10]{}[01] = \underline{0.121306 }
\eears
Ans: $0.606531 / (0.606531 + 0.121306)  = \boxed{0.8333}$



\esoln}
