\JGWitem{Course M, Nov 2006, \#32}
For an aggregate loss distribution S: 
\begin{enumerate}
\item The number of claims has a negative binomial distribution with $r = 16$ and $\beta= 6.$
\item The claim amounts are uniformly distributed on the interval $(0, 8).$
\item The number of claims and claim amounts are mutually independent. 
\end{enumerate}
Using the normal approximation for aggregate losses, calculate the premium such that the probability that aggregate losses will exceed the premium is 5\%

\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 500 
\item[(B)] 520 
\item[(C)] 540 
\item[(D)] 560 
\item[(E)] 580 
\end{description}}

\leaveit{\bsoln
\bears
   \Expect{N} &=& r\beta = 96 \\
   \Var{N} &=& r\beta(1+\beta) = 672 \\
   \Expect{X} &=& (b+a)/2 = 4 \\
   \Var{X} &=& (b-a)^2/12 = 16/3 \\
   \mu = \Expect{S} &=& \Expect{N}\Expect{X} = 96\times 4 = 384 \\
   \sigma^2 = \Var{S} &=& \Var{N}\Expect{X}^2 + \Var{X}\Expect{N} \\
           &=& 672\times 4^2 + (16/3)\times 96 = 11,264 = (106.132)^2 \\
   \Prob{S\geq \pi} &=& 0.05 \\
   \Prob{\frac{S-\mu}{\sigma}\leq \frac{\pi-\mu}{\sigma}} &=& 0.95 \\
   \pi &=& \mu + \sigma\Phi^{-1}(0.95) = 384 + 106.132\times 1.64485 = 558.57
\eears
\underline{Ans: D}

\esoln}
