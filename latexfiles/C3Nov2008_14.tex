\JGWitem{Nov 2008 \#14}
You are given this table:
 \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|} \hline
   Age(x)   &   $l_x$    &    $d_x$    \\ \hline
     0      &            &   100       \\ \hline
     1      &            &             \\ \hline
     2      &            &             \\ \hline
     3      &            &             \\ \hline
     4      &    125     &   75        \\ \hline
     5      &            &   50   \\  \hline
\end{tabular}

You are also given:
\bears
  \qx[3|2]{1} &=& 0.329, \\
  \px[3]{0} &=& 0.427
\eears
Caclulate the number of lives in the cohort at age 3.
\showsol{\bsoln
  \bears 
    l_5 &=& l_4 - d_4 = 125-75 = 50 \\
    l_6 &=& l_5 - d_5 = 50-50 = 0 \quad \underline{\mbox{ so }\omega = 6}, \\ \\ 
    0.329 &=& \prepostsub{3|2}{q}{1} = \dfrac{\ell_4-\ell_6}{\ell_1} = \frac{\ell_4}{\ell_1} = \frac{\ell_4}{\ell_0-d_0}
    = \frac{125}{l_0-100} \quad\quad\quad\Longrightarrow \underline{\ell_0 = 480 } \\
    0.427 &=& \prepostsub{3}{p}{0} = \frac{l_3}{l_0} \quad\Longrightarrow \underline{l_3 = 205} 
    \quad\quad\quad\quad\boxed{205 \text{ alive at age 3}}
  \eears 
\esoln}
