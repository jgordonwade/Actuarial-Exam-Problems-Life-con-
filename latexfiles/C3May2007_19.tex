\JGWitem{C3 May 2007, 19}
For whole life insurance of 1 on (40), you are given: 
\begin{enumerate}
\item $i= 0.06 $
\item $p_{50} = p_{51} = p_{52}$ 
\item The net premium policy values at durations 10 and 13 are equal.
\item $\ddot{a}_{50} =10.0 $
\end{enumerate}
Calculate $p_{50}$.
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 0.942 
\item[(B)] 0.946 
\item[(C)] 0.950 
\item[(D)] 0.954 
\item[(E)] 0.958 
\end{description}}

\showsol{\bsoln
Assuming a level net premium of $\pi$, we have
\[ _t\!V = \Ax{40+t}-\pi\ax**{40+t} = \left(1-d\ax**{40+t}\right) - \pi\ax**{40+t} = 1+(d+\pi)\ax**{40+t}. \]
Because the net premium policy values at durations 10 and 13 are equal, we have
 $_{10}\!V  = _{13}\!V$ means $1+(d+\pi)\ax**{50} = 1+(d+\pi)\ax**{53}$ which means $\underline{\ax**{50}=\ax**{53}}.$

\medskip
Since $p_{50} = p_{51} = p_{52}$ we have $\px[2]{50}=(\px{50})^2$ and $\px[3]{50}=(\px{50})^3$, so that
\bears  
    \ax**{50} &=& 1 + v\px{50}\ax**{51}  \\
                   &=& 1 + v\px{50}\left(1+v\px{51}\ax**{52} \right) \\
                   &=& 1 + v\px{50}\left(1+v\px{51}\left[1+v\px{52}\ax**{53} \right]\right) \\
   \ax**{50}  &=& 1 + v\px{50} + \left(v\px{50}\right)^2 + \left(v\px{50}\right)^3\ax**{53}. 
\eears
Since $\ax**{50}=10=\ax**{53},$ the above equation means
\bears
   10  &=& 1 + v\px{50} + \left(v\px{50}\right)^2 + 10\left(v\px{50}\right)^3 \\
   10(1-z^3)  &=& 1 + z + z^2, \quad\quad \text{where z}=v\px{50} \\
    10(1-z^3) &=& \dfrac{1-z^3}{1-z} + 10z^3 \\
    10(1-z^3) &=& \dfrac{1-z^3}{1-z} \\
    10 &=& \dfrac{1}{1-z} \quad\quad\Longrightarrow\quad z=\dfrac{9}{10} \quad\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \px{50}=\dfrac{1.06\cdot 9}{10} = \boxed{0.954.}
\eears
\esoln}
