\JGWitem{May 2005 \#19}
 A discrete probability distribution has the following properties:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $p_k=c\bigl(1+{1\over k}\bigr)p_{k-1}$.
\item $p_0 = 0.5$ 
\end{enumerate}
Calculate c.
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 0.06
\item[(B)] 0.13
\item[(C)] 0.29
\item[(D)] 0.35
\item[(E)] 0.40
\end{description}}
\showsol{\bsoln
   We immediately see that this distribution is an $(a,b,0)$ distribution with $a=b=c$.
   Inspection of the possibilities in the Tables provided shows that the distribution is the
   Negative Binomial with $r=2$ and
   \[  p_0 = 0.5 = \dfrac{\beta}{1+\beta}, \]
   so that $\beta=\sqrt{2}-1$ and $c=\dfrac{\sqrt{2}-1}{\sqrt{2}} = 0.2929.$
\esoln}
