\JGWitem{C3 May 2013 \#11} % LTAM Sample (nov 2019) $ #8.26
For a one-year term insurance on (45), whose mortality follows a double decrement model, you are given:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] The death benefit for cause (1) is 1000 and for cause (2) is $F.$
\item[(ii)] Death benefits are payable at the end of the year of death.
\item[(iii)] $\qx{45}[(1)]=0.04$ and $\qx{45}[(2)]=0.20$
\item[(iv)] $i=0.06$
\item[(v)] $Z$ is the present value random variable for this insurance.
\end{itemize}
Calculate the value of $F$ that minimizes $\Var{Z}$
\showsol{\bsoln
A value of $F$ minimizes $\Var{Z}$ if and only if it minimizes $\Var{\tilde{Z}}$ where $\tilde{Z}=1.06\cdot Z,$ and since 
$Z$ is the present value random variable for this insurance,
\bears
    \tilde{Z} &=& \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} 1000 & \text{w/ probability }0.04 \\ F & \text{w/ probability }0.2 \\ 0 & \text{w/ probability }0.76. \end{array}\right. \\
    \Expect{\tilde{Z}} &=&  1000\cdot 0.04 + 0.2\cdot F = 40 + \dfrac{F}{5}, \\
    \Expect{\tilde{Z}^2} &=&  1000^2\cdot 0.04 + 0.2\cdot F^2 = 40,000 + \dfrac{F^2}{5}, \\
    \Var{\tilde{Z}} &=& \left(40,000 + \dfrac{F^2}{5} \right) - \left( 40 + \dfrac{F}{5} \right)^2 = \dfrac{4}{25} \left(F^2 - 100 F + 240000\right)
\eears
so $\Var{Z}$ is \fbox{minimized when $F=50$}. 
\\ \esoln}
