\JGWitem{Nov 2012 \#11}
For a whole life insurance of 1000 on (70), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Death benefits are payable at the end of the year of death.
\item Mortality follows the Illustrative Life Table.
\item \begin{tabular}[t]{|c|c|} \hline Maturity (in years) & Annual effective spot rate \\ \hline 1 & 1.6\% \\ \hline 2 & 2.6\% \\ \hline \end{tabular}
\item For the year starting at time $k -1$ and ending at time $k$, $k = 3, 4,5, \ldots,$ the one-year forward rate is 6\%.
\end{enumerate}
Calculate the expected present value of the death benefits.

\showsol{\bsoln
 Let $A$ denote the EPV of the death benefits, and let $v(k)$ denote the present-value discount factor for a payment $k$ years from now. 
From the problem data, we obtain
\bear
     v(1) &=& 1.016^{-1} \nonumber \\
     v(2) &=& 1.026^{-2} \nonumber \\
     v(j) &=& \dfrac{1}{1.026^2\cdot 1.06^{j-2}} = v(2)v^{j-2} \quad\text{ for }j\geq 3, \text{ where }v=\frac{1}{1.06} \label{C3Nov2012_11_a}
\eear
So,
\bears
     \frac{A}{1000} &=& \sum_{k=0}^\infty v(k+1) \prepostsub{k|}{q}{70} \\
        &=& v(1) q_{70} + v(2) \prepostsub{1|}{q}{70} + \sum_{k=2}^\infty v(k+1) \prepostsub{k|}{q}{70} \\
           && \quad      (\text{change of indices: }j=k-2) \\
        &=& v(1) q_{70} + v(2) \prepostsub{1|}{q}{70} + \prepostsub{2}{p}{70}v(2)\sum_{j=0}^\infty v^{j+1} \prepostsub{j|}{q}{72} \\
        &=& v(1) q_{70} + v(2) \prepostsub{1|}{q}{70} + \prepostsub{2}{p}{70}v(2)A_{70}
\eears
where we've used (\ref{C3Nov2012_11_a}) and the fact that
$\prepostsub{(j+2)|}{q}{70} = \prepostsub{2}{p}{70}\prepostsub{j|}{q}{72}$, and where $A_{72}$ is as in the Illustrative Life Table. 

\medskip
From the Illustrative Life Table we find:
\bears
   q_{70} = 0.03318, \quad   \prepostsub{1|}{q}{70} = 0.03506, \quad    \prepostsub{2}{q}{70} = 0.93176, \quad\text{ and }A_{72} = 0.54560, 
\eears
and so
\[
     \boxed{A = 548.889}
\]



\esoln}
