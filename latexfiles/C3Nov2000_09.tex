\JGWitem{Nov 2000, \#9}
For a special whole life insurance of 100,000 on (x), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\delta = 0.06$
\item The death benefit is payable at the moment of death.
\item If death occurs by accident during the first 30 years, the death benefit is doubled.
\item $\mu_x^{(\tau)}(t) = 0.008$ for $t\geq 0$.
\item $\mu_x^{(1)}(t) = 0.001$ for $t\geq 0$, where $\mu_x^{(1)}$ is the force of decrement due to death by accident.
\end{enumerate}
Calculate the single benefit premium for this insurance.
\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 12,195
\item[(B)] 12,622
\item[(C)] 13,044
\item[(D)] 13,141
\item[(E)] 13,235
\end{description}}
\showsol{\bsoln
\showMC{\underline{ANS: (C)} \\}
 Model this insurance as (whole life insurance) + (30-year accident term insurance). 

\medskip
In general, the APV for an insurance covering times $t$ from 0 to $n$ we have
\[
   A = \Expect{e^{-\delta T}} = \int_0^n e^{-\delta t}f_T(t)\,dt.
\]
For the whole-life insurance we have $n=\infty$ and $f_T(t) = \mu^{(\tau)}  e^{-\mu^{(\tau)}},$ and \\
for the term accident insurance we have $n=30$ and $f_T(t) = \mu^{(1)} e^{-\mu^{(\tau)}}.$ So,
\bears
   A &=& \left(10^5\int_0^\infty \left(e^{-\delta t}\right)\left(\mu^{(\tau)}e^{-\mu^{(\tau)}}\right)\,dt \right)
        + \left(10^5\int_0^{30} \left(e^{-\delta t}\right)\left(\mu^{(1)}e^{-\mu^{(\tau)}}\right)\,dt \right) \\
     &=& 10^5\cdot\left(\frac{\mu^{(\tau)}}{\mu^{(\tau)} + \delta} \;+\;\frac{\mu^{(1)}(1-e^{-30(\mu^{(\tau)}+\delta)})}{\mu^{(\tau)}+\delta}\right) \\
     &=& 10^5\cdot\left(\frac{0.008}{0.068} + \frac{0.001\cdot(1-e^{-(30)\cdot(0.068)})}{0.068}\right) \\
    &=& 13044.08
\eears
\esoln}
