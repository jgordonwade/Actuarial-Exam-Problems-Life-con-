\JGWitem{Course M, Nov 2006, \#9}
A casino has a game that makes payouts at a Poisson rate of 5 per hour and the payout amounts are 1, 2, 3, \ldots, without limt. 
The probability that any given Payout is equal to $i$ is $1/2^i$. Payouts are independent. 

\medskip
Calculate the probability that there are no payouts of 1, 2, or 3 in a given 20 minute period. 

\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 0.08 
\item[(B)] 0.13 
\item[(C)] 0.18 
\item[(D)] 0.23 
\item[(E)] 0.28 
\end{description}}


\leaveit{\bsoln
The game makes payouts of amount $i$ at a Poisson rate of $\lambda_i$ per hour, where $\lambda_i=5/2^i$. The 
probability we're looking for is
\[
   \left(e^{-\lambda_1/3}\right)\cdot\left(e^{-\lambda_2/3}\right)\cdot\left(e^{-\lambda_3/3}\right) 
    = e^{-(\lambda_1+\lambda_2+\lambda_3)/3} = e^{-35/24} \approx 0.2326
\]
\underline{Ans D}

\esoln}
