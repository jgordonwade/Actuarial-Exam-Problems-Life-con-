\JGWitem{Nov 2004, \#9}
For a special fully continuous last survivor insurance of 1 on (x) and (y), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T_x$ and $T_y$ are independent.
\item $\mu_{x+t} = 0.08$ for $t > 0$, 
\item $\mu_{y+t} = 0.04$ for $t > 0$, 
\item $\delta = 0.06$
\item $\pi$ is the annual benefit premium payable until the 
  first of (x) and (y) dies.
\end{enumerate}
Calculate $\pi$.
\showsol{\bsoln
Because $\pi$ is the benefit premium, it is computed from the equivalance principle:
\[
  0 = \overline{A}_{\overline{xy}} - \pi\overline{a}_{xy} 
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \pi = \frac{\overline{A}_{\overline{xy}}}{\overline{a}_{xy}}
\]
Use:
\bears
  \overline{A}_{\overline{xy}} + \overline{A}_{xy} &=& \overline{A}_{x} + \overline{A}_{x} \\
  \overline{A}_{xy} &=& \frac{\mu}{\mu+\delta} \quad\mbox{ where }\mu = \mu_x + \mu_y, \\
  \overline{a}_{xy} &=& \frac{1-\overline{A}_{xy}}{\delta}
\eears
\underline{ANS:} 0.054857

\esoln}
