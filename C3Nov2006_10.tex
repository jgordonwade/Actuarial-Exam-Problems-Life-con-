\JGWitem{Course M, Nov 2006, \#10}
You arrive at a subway station at 6:15. Until 7:00, trains arrive at a Poisson rate of 1 train per 30 minutes. Starting at 7:00, they arrive at a Poisson rate of 2 trains per 30 minutes. 

\medskip
Calculate your expected waiting time until a train arrives. 

\showMC{\begin{description}
\item[(A)] 24 minutes 
\item[(B)] 25 minutes 
\item[(C)] 26 minutes 
\item[(D)] 27 minutes 
\item[(E)] 28 minutes 
\end{description}}

\leaveit{\bsoln
Let $T$ be the waiting time for the first train. We have
\[
  S_T(t) = \Prob{T>t} = \left\{\begin{array}{ll}
        e^{-t/30} & \mbox{ for }t\leq 45 \\
        e^{-45/30}\times e^{-(t-45)/15} & \mbox{ otherwise,}
     \end{array}\right.
\]
and so 
\bears
   \Expect{T} &=& \int_0^\infty S_T(t) \,dt \\
              &=& \int_0^{45} e^{-t/30}\,dt 
                + e^{-45/30}\int_{45}^{\infty} e^{-(t-45)/15} \,dt = 30-15e^{-1.5} \approx 26.653.
\eears
\underline{Ans: D}

\esoln}
