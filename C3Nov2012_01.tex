\JGWitem{Nov 2012, \#1}
For two lives, (80) and (90), with independent future lifetimes, you are given:
\begin{center}\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|} \hline
      $k$   &  $p_{80+k}$   &   $p_{90+k}$ \\ \hline
       0    &   0.9        &   0.6 \\ \hline
       1    &   0.8        &   0.5 \\ \hline
       2    &   0.7        &   0.4 \\ \hline
\end{tabular}\end{center}
Calculate the probability that the last survivor will die in the third year.

\showsol{\bsoln
\bears
   \prepostsub{2|}{q}{\overline{xy}} &=& \prepostsub{2|}{q}{x} + \prepostsub{2|}{q}{y} - \prepostsub{2|}{q}{xy} \\
      &=& \prepostsub{2}{p}{x}q_{x+2} + \prepostsub{2}{p}{y}q_{y+2} - \prepostsub{2}{p}{xy}q_{x+2:y+2}  \\
      &=& \prepostsub{2}{p}{x}(1-p_{x+2}) + \prepostsub{2}{p}{x}(1-p_{x+2}) - \prepostsub{2}{p}{xy}(1-p_{x+2:y+2})  \\
      &=& \prepostsub{2}{p}{x}(1-p_{x+2}) + \prepostsub{2}{p}{y}(1-p_{y+2}) - \prepostsub{2}{p}{x}\prepostsub{2}{p}{y}\left(1-(p_{x+2})(p_{y+2})\right)  \\
\eears
\bears
   \prepostsub{2}{p}{x} &=& p_xp_{x+1} = 0.72 \\
   \prepostsub{2}{p}{y} &=& p_yp_{y+1} = 0.30 
\eears

\eears
