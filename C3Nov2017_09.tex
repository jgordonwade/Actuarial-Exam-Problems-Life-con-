\JGWitem{C3 Nov 2017, \#9}
For a special fully discrete 5-year deferred 3-year term insurance of 100,000 on (x), you are given:
\begin{enumerate}
\item There are two premium payments, each equal to P . The first is paid at the
beginning of the first year and the second is paid at the end of the 5-year
deferral period.
\item The following probabilities:
    \[ \prepostsub{5}{p}{x} = 0.95, 
\quad\quad q_{x+5}=0.02, \quad\quad q_{x+6}=0.03, \quad\quad q_{x+7}=0.04. \]
\item $i=0.06$
\end{enumerate}
Calculate P using the equivalence principle.
\showsol{\bsoln
\bears
    0 &=& 10^5\Bigl[ v^6\prepostsub{5|}{q}{x} + v^7\prepostsub{6|}{q}{x} + v^8\prepostsub{7|}{q}{x} \Bigr] - P\Bigl[1 + \prepostsub{5}{E}{x}\Bigr] \\
\eears
\bears
    \prepostsub{5|}{q}{x} &=& \prepostsub{5}{p}{x}\cdot q_{x+5} = 0.019 \\
    \prepostsub{6|}{q}{x} &=& \prepostsub{5}{p}{x}\cdot p_{x+5}\cdot q_{x+6} = 0.02793 \\
    \prepostsub{7|}{q}{x} &=& \prepostsub{5}{p}{x}\cdot p_{x+5} \cdot p_{x+6}\cdot q_{x+7} = 0.0361228
\eears
\bears
0 &=& 10^5\Bigl[ \dfrac{0.019}{1.06^6} + \dfrac{0.02793}{1.06^7} + \dfrac{0.0361228}{1.06^8} \Bigr] - P\cdot \Bigl[1+\dfrac{0.95}{1.06^5} \Bigr]\\
 && P = 10^5\cdot \frac{0.0546\,3319}{1.7098\,9526} = \boxed{3195.12}
\eears
\esoln}
